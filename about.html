environmental impact

With the current increase of bee colony collapse disorder, it has become clearer than ever the major role bee plays in our ecosystem and agriculture. Honeybee colonies have been on the decline for decades. With 5 million managed honeybee colonies in the 1904s to about 2.66 million today. Without bees many plants would die off without human intervention or have drastically reduced pollination of plants. This would lead to a rippled effect down the food chain. Many other species would either die off or have reduced in numbers. Without bees, there would be a decline in fresh products, and human nutrition would suffer. Health issues would increase in the population and life expectancy would decline. Many plants would need to be hand pollinated to survive and would most like die off without human intervention, coursing many species to loss their food source and major food chain collapse.
